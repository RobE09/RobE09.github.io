The Companions of Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door

Perhaps one of the greatest video games, not only of its era but of all time. The sequel to the original Paper Mario, the fan favorite game, Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door, is so well regarded by fans for many reasons. So often, Nintendo games are driven purely by gameplay instead of a complicated story. Still, the Paper Mario series tends to explore a more compelling storyline than many other Nintendo games. Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door is a beloved classic of the Nintendo GameCube era, released in 2004. It follows the story of Mario as he embarks on a quest to find the seven Crystal Stars, which will help him save Princess Peach and prevent the shadowy organization known as the X-Nauts from taking over the world. Along the way, Mario is joined by a colorful cast of companions, each with unique abilities and personalities. These companions play a crucial role in the gameplay and the narrative, making them an essential part of what makes Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door such a memorable experience. 

The first companion that Mario meets is Goombella, a spunky young Goomba who becomes his sidekick early in the game. Goombella serves as both a tutorial guide and a source of comic relief, providing Mario with helpful tips and humorous commentary on the various locations they visit. She also can tattle on enemies, revealing their weaknesses and strategies. This makes her a valuable asset in battles and a fun and engaging character to interact with.

Another early companion is Koops, a timid Koopa who initially joins Mario out of obligation to his deceased father, who once served as a hero. Koops can hide in his shell, which is used defensively and offensively in battles. He also has a personal story arc that involves him gaining confidence and learning to stand up for himself, which adds depth to his character and makes him more relatable.

Madame Flurrie is the third companion to Mario. She is introduced as a dramatic, high-class performer who is infatuated with Mario after he helps her by finding her lost necklace. Once she joins Mario and the rest of his companions, Madame Flurrie proves to be both a strong character and a powerful ally, harnessing the power of the wind to reveal new passages in the world and to blow away crowds of enemies.

My personal favorite companion is the baby Yoshi that the player can hatch themselves. The color depends on how long it’ll take to hatch. Yoshi is a ridable companion, allowing for quicker traveling. Yoshi is also a powerhouse in combat, and a cute party member. This Yoshi is definitely one of the best companions in the game.

One of the most unique and memorable companions is Vivian, a member of the Shadow Sirens, a group of powerful witches who serve as the game's main antagonists. Vivian initially appears as an enemy but joins Mario's party after a misunderstanding. Vivian can turn invisible and use fire-based attacks, making her a versatile and valuable battle ally. Her back story involves her struggling to find acceptance among her sisters and discovering her own identity, which adds a layer of complexity to her character.

Another standout companion is Bobbery, an elderly Bob-omb retired from his days as a sailor. Bobbery is initially reluctant to join Mario's quest but eventually decides to come out of retirement to help save the world. Bobbery can use explosives in battles, which can damage enemies. He also has a tragic backstory involving the loss of his wife, which adds emotional weight to his character and makes him one of the more memorable companions.

Finally, there is Ms. Mowz, a thief who initially steals from Mario but later joins his party. Ms. Mowz can steal items and coins from enemies in battles, making her a valuable asset for resource management. She also has a playful and flirtatious personality, adding fun and lightheartedness to the otherwise serious story.

Each of these companions brings something unique to the table regarding gameplay mechanics and narrative depth. They all have their own stories and struggles, explored through optional side quests and conversations. This makes the game more engaging and immersive and adds to the sense of camaraderie and teamwork at the core of Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door's gameplay. Overall, the companions in Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door are an essential part of what makes the game so beloved. They are all well-designed, with unique personalities and abilities that complement each other and add variety to battles. They also have their own stories and struggles, which add depth and emotional weight to the game's narrative. Without these companions, the game would not be the same, and it is a testament to the game's design that even a game with such silly visuals as this one could take its players on such an enjoyable narrative journey.
